Lodhi Garden, New Delhi

Lodhi Garden is a serene green space nestled in the heart of New Delhi, India. Known for its historical tombs and botanical variety, it is a perfect example of Mughal-era landscaping. The garden is an oasis for both locals and tourists looking to connect with nature and history. Covering an area of 90 acres, it boasts lush lawns, lakes, and a variety of plants.

Lalbagh Botanical Garden, Bengaluru

Lalbagh Botanical Garden is located in the heart of Bengaluru, Karnataka. It spans over 240 acres and houses more than 1,000 species of plants. Established in the 18th century, the garden is home to rare plant species and is known for its annual flower shows. Its iconic glasshouse is modeled after the Crystal Palace of London and attracts thousands of visitors each year.

Jawaharlal Nehru Botanical Garden, Gangtok

The Jawaharlal Nehru Botanical Garden in Gangtok, Sikkim, is a haven for nature lovers and plant enthusiasts. The garden is situated at an altitude of about 1,600 meters and spans 10 hectares of lush greenery. It is home to a rich variety of flora, showcasing both native and exotic species. Acharya J.C.B Indian Botanic Garden, Kolkata

The Acharya Jagadish Chandra Bose Indian Botanic Garden, located in Howrah, Kolkata, is one of the most renowned botanical gardens in India. It is famous for its rich collection of plants, trees, and diverse flora. This garden, established in 1787, serves as a major center for research in botany and plant sciences.
